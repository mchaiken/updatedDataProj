Conclusion

From this data we can see that about half of the states with below average scores from the Brady Campaign also have above average rates of murder with a firearm. This isn't huge, but does suggest that there is at least some sort of correlation between strength of gun control and muder rate with a firearm. It should also be taken into account that in many places with stronger gun control, weapons are brought in from out of state illegally, and the death by firearm rate in the US is over 40 times that of the UK. Either way, to suggest that these numbers should be dismissed or that there is no relation to the number of people who are killed by guns is untrue.
