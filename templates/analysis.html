Conclusion

From this data we can see that about half of the states with bellow average scores from the Brady Campaign also have above average rates of murder with a fire arm.This isn't huge, but does suggest that there is at least some sort of coorilation between strength of gun control and muder rate with a fire arm. It should also be taken into account that in many places with stronger gun control, weapons are brough in from out of state illegally, and the death by fire arm rate in the US is over 40 times that of the UK. Either way, to suggest that these numbers should be dismissed or that there is no relation to the number of people who are killed by guns is untrue.
